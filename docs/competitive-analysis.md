# Competitive Analysis

Current snapshot: 2026-05-24 JST. Re-check Steam pages before public positioning.

## Reference Categories

| Category | Lesson |
| --- | --- |
| Cooperative survival | Players need shared pressure and clear short-term tasks |
| Social deduction | Suspicion needs evidence, not random accusation |
| Proximity voice games | Audio range and occlusion create stories |
| Session-based horror | Short matches support repeated friend-group play |
| Community-server games | Server ownership reduces official operating load |

## Similarity Risk

The project must not be sold as a clone or direct successor. The distinct expression is:

- Near-future fictional metal research vessel instead of 19th century expedition ship.
- Hull, fuel, radio, route, power, heat, flooding, and ice pressure instead of occult survival horror.
- Agents/saboteurs instead of magical traitors.
- Final navigation and rescue route instead of copied final objective structure.
- Duty records, radio logs, chart notes, and tool marks instead of only social accusation.

## Market Assumptions To Validate

- Friend-group price sensitivity is high.
- Chinese, English, and Japanese localization matter early.
- Moderation quality affects reviews quickly.
- Playtest access control matters more than a large open beta at first.
- Dedicated/community hosting matters if official servers are attacked or too costly.
